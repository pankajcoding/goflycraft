Installation

Installation Aspects

You can refer to BL-Pegson’s technical department for advice on installation design or any other aspect relating to the ‘M’ Series Jaw Crusher. Access is required all around the machine for inspection and maintenance of the crusher. Fixed installations usually provide a lifting beam with hoist over the crusher for maintenance purposes, principally used when changing the manganese wear parts. These should allow for sufficient over capacity (see weights - Section 2.2.6) and height for the major machine assemblies to be lifted, moved to the side and dropped down to floor level.

The crusher is supplied with a standard vee grooved flywheel. The purchaser is to supply a suitable wedge belt drive, drive guard, motor pulley and main drive motor. Details of motor sizes for each machine are contained in the product literature, however we recommend the purchaser consults BL-Pegson Limited for specific advice prior to installation. Motor speed and pulley diameter should be selected to give recommended crusher speed.

Flywheel guards should be constructed to incorporate mesh ‘windows’ to enable the correct alignment of the flywheel locking mechanisms to be easily checked at all times. Where guards are provided they are so designed and manufactured to ensure so far as reasonably practicable that the machinery and plant on which they are fitted can be operated safely and without risk to health when properly used. However, the Company cannot guarantee that the guards provided will meet the requirements laid down by individual Inspectors and any additional guard and/ or modification to guarding supplied, which may be required for any reason whatsoever, will be charged as an addition to the contract price.

Every endeavour will be made to supply equipment as specified, but we reserve the right, where necessary, to amend specifications without prior notice as we operate a policy of continual product development.

Advice can also be given on the design of feed chutes, discharge chutes and discharge conveyors to enhance the installation and performance of your BL-Pegson Crusher.

BL-Pegson dealer’s engineer is to be employed to commission the machine when the installation is complete and ready to run and crush stone. The following notes regarding installation can only be general owing to the variable nature of plant installations. Always feel free to contact your local dealer or BL-Pegson for guidance on any aspect.

1 Preparing the Machine

Remove any temporary sealings added for storage purposes and clean the factory applied protective coatings from machined surfaces. Visually check the machine for any damage, and ensure that all components are clean and free of any obstructions.

2 Siting the Machine

Confirm that the foundations which are to support the machine and ancillary equipment are firm and level and are able to support the plant when in use. Ascertain that adequate space is available above, around and below the machine for feed and discharge, and access for servicing purposes. Consult the General Arrangement drawing (supplied at time of order) for loadings, critical dimensions, clearances, etc.

Ensure also that suitable shims are available for levelling purposes, when these are necessary. The following points should be noted:-

1. A variable speed feeder complete with a suitably sized hopper should be sited prior to the crusher to regulate the feed material into the machine.

2. Feed chutes should be easily remov- able, complete with fixings and sup- ports, to provide access to the machine.

3. The discharged material normally flows vertically from the machine via a dis- charge chute onto a conveyor; the discharge chute should be constructed from a material and to a design which allows the discharge to flow freely. Any restriction in the discharge can cause build-up of material in the crushing chamber, and possible resultant damage to the machine. The discharge conveyor must be interlocked with the machine so that in the event of a conveyor stop- page, the material feed to the machine also stops.

As with all crushing equipment the feed area of the crusher needs to be carefully designed since this is potentially a very dangerous area. Overspill of the material feed must be avoided.

3 Installing the Machine

Lift the machine via the four lifting lugs on the frame (Figure 2e) using chains and suitable lifting equipment. Remove the timber packings used to support the machine whilst in transit and any chocks in the crushing chamber.

When lifting the machine or any part of the machine it is the responsibility of the customer to ensure that both the crane and the lifting tackle are suitable and have the correct safe working load. Section 4.2 gives the weights of the complete machine and also part assemblies.
warning

Level the machine in both directions by using a spirit level positioned on the main frame. Fix the live frame securely and grout or shim it to its foundation (of concrete or steel beams). Install the drive motor (BL-Pegson or another manufacturer’s supply) and vee belts following the relevant manufacturer’s instructions. Ensure that the direction of the motor is to drive the flywheels towards the crushing chamber (when viewed from above). Contact BL-Pegson Limited if a combustion engine drive is envisaged.

Check the proper tension of the vee belts and alignment of the drive motor ensuring that the flwheels and drive motor shaft are in-line.
