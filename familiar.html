1.2 Operating principles of a cone crusher

In a cone crusher, the process material is crushed between two rigid surfaces. The motion of the moving surface is independent of the load on the crusher. The crushing action is achieved by an eccentric gyratory movement of the mainshaft. Individual pieces of process material are nipped, compressed and crushed between the mantle and the concave ring.

The quality and quantity of the produced process material is a product of the interaction between the crusher and the process material. The crushing chamber geometry, the crusher's dynamics, and the quality of the process material are the most important factors.

The concave, mantle, eccentric assembly and the location of the pivot point determine the chamber geometry of the cone crusher. The crusher's drive motor rotates the eccentric assembly at a constant speed through a gear transmission. The rotation of the eccentric assembly causes the lower end of the main shaft to gyrate. At the top of the mainshaft is the spider bearing that acts as the pivot point for this gyrating motion.

The mantle is fixed to the mainshaft and the concave is fixed to the topshell. The gyration of the mainshaft means that the distance between the mantle and any given point on the concave continual, varies. When this distance decreases the incoming process material will be subjected to a compressing stress and will be crushed. When the mantle moves away from the concave, the process material is able to flow down through the chamber.

The mantle and the concave must be replaced regularly as they are subject to continuous wear. The rate of wear depends on the characteristics of the process material.

Setting adjustment

To control fragment size and to compensate for wear the crusher has a setting control system, called the hydraulic set system. It basically consists of a robust hydraulic jack which supports the main shaft and adjusts its position. Moving the hydraulic set piston up and down will change the discharge setting and the size of the crushed material.

Setting decrease = smaller fragments/setting increase = larger fragments

Auxiliary systems

The cone crusher has oil circulatory system for lubrication of the main shaft.

Glossary of DC-900 cone terms

CSS- Close side setting
OSS- Open side setting
TPH- Ton per hour
Crushing chamber- The region between the manganese (concave and mantle) where crushing takes place.
Manganese -The cone mantle and concave crushing members.
Stroke- Difference of maximum and minimum distance between bottom of concave and mantle during one revolution of eccentric shaft.
Well graded feed- An even distribution of material particle sizes in the feed.
Segregated feed- A poorly mixed, uneven distribution particle sizes in the feed.
Wear parts- Components subject to wear from the stone being crushed.
Liners- The manganese components which perform the crushing are commonly referred as liners.
Choke feed- The feed rate to the machine that enables optimum crushing performance to take place.
Capacity - The through put of crusher in tons per hour.
Product size- The size of fragments after been crushed.
